## 2 · Artist & Project Framing

Hari Tris is a multidisciplinary artist born in Vietnam and based in Singapore. She grew up drifting to sleep amid her father's midnight composing sessions — old Vietnamese love songs that formed silent images before she had words for them.

*LUCID DRIFT* is an ongoing experimental project exploring **memory, fragmentation, and reconstruction** through sound, visuals, and interaction. It is situated within a broader research frame — **“Orphaned Archives”** — which questions ownership, authorship, and the survival of memory after death, particularly outside centralized digital systems.

---

## 3 · Thematic Framing — Orphaned Archives

> When a person dies, who owns their data?

An *orphaned archive* is a condition in which memory persists without clear authorship, ownership, or system of care.

*LUCID DRIFT* examines the tension between:

- **analog memory** — cassette tapes, roneo prints, handwritten scores  
- **digital infrastructures** that render these formats obsolete  

Against the backdrop of platform capitalism, nostalgia is routinely digitized, compressed, and consumed. This project resists that cycle, proposing an **alternative archival logic** — one that is unstable, embodied, and non-extractive.

It asks:

> Can memory exist outside the extractive logic of centralized platforms?

---

## 4 · Personal Origin — The Father's Archive

The foundation of the project is a personal sound archive left by the artist's father, **Nguyễn Thanh Tịnh (1950–2004)** — a Vietnamese composer whose work bridges traditional Vietnamese musical sensibilities and early modern *Tân Nhạc* influences.

Born during a period of historical and cultural transition in post-colonial Vietnam, his music carries emotional conditions shaped by displacement, intimacy, and impermanence. His compositions survive across hybrid formats — cassette recordings, handwritten scores, analog traces — situated outside any stable digital preservation system.

The project emerges from the experience of losing him at an early age and later returning to these fragmented materials without a singular narrative or authoritative structure. Rather than reconstructing biography, *LUCID DRIFT* engages with the archive as **an unstable carrier of memory**, where absence becomes a generative condition for interpretation, recomposition, and interaction.

The archive is not treated as a completed body of work, but as an **incomplete and orphaned system of memory.**

---

## 5 · Du Tử Ca — The Legacy Anchor

Among these materials, one defining work operates as both **first major artistic statement** and **final crystallization** of the composer's legacy: *Du Tử Ca*.

The title, derived from Sino-Vietnamese (Hán–Việt), carries layered meaning:

- **Du (遊)** — to wander, to drift freely  
- **Tử (子)** — a person, scholar, or wandering subject  
- **Ca (歌)** — song, chant  

Together, *Du Tử Ca* evokes a **“song of the wandering soul.”**

Within the project, it functions as a **threshold object**:
- closure of authorship  
- dispersal of identity  
- prefiguration of fragmentation  

> memory does not resolve, but continuously disperses into fragments of repetition, loss, and re-emergence.
