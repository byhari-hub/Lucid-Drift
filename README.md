## 2 · Artist & Project Framing

Hari Tris is a multidisciplinary artist born in Vietnam and based in Singapore. She grew up drifting to sleep amid her father's midnight composing sessions — old Vietnamese love songs that formed silent images before she had words for them.

*LUCID DRIFT* is an ongoing experimental project exploring **memory, fragmentation, and reconstruction** through sound, visuals, and interaction. It is situated within a broader research frame — **“Orphaned Archives”** — which questions ownership, authorship, and the survival of memory after death, particularly outside centralized digital systems.

---

## 3 · Thematic Framing — Orphaned Archives

> When a person dies, who owns their data?

An *orphaned archive* is a condition in which memory persists without clear authorship, ownership, or system of care.

*LUCID DRIFT* examines the tension between:

- **analog memory** — cassette tapes, roneo prints, handwritten scores
- **digital infrastructures** that render these formats obsolete

Against the backdrop of platform capitalism, nostalgia is routinely digitized, compressed, and consumed. This project resists that cycle, proposing an **alternative archival logic** — one that is unstable, embodied, and non-extractive.

It asks:

> Can memory exist outside the extractive logic of centralized platforms?

---

## 4 · Personal Origin — The Father's Archive

The foundation of the project is a personal sound archive left by the artist's father, **Nguyễn Thanh Tịnh (1950–2004)** — a Vietnamese composer whose work bridges traditional Vietnamese musical sensibilities and early modern *Tân Nhạc* influences.

Born during a period of historical and cultural transition in post-colonial Vietnam, his music carries emotional conditions shaped by displacement, intimacy, and impermanence. His compositions survive across hybrid formats — cassette recordings, handwritten scores, analog traces — situated outside any stable digital preservation system.

The project emerges from the experience of losing him at an early age and later returning to these fragmented materials without a singular narrative or authoritative structure. Rather than reconstructing biography, *LUCID DRIFT* engages with the archive as **an unstable carrier of memory**, where absence becomes a generative condition for interpretation, recomposition, and interaction.

The archive is not treated as a completed body of work, but as an **incomplete and orphaned system of memory.**

---

## 5 · Du Tử Ca — The Legacy Anchor

Among these materials, one defining work operates as both **first major artistic statement** and **final crystallization** of the composer's legacy: *Du Tử Ca*.

The title, derived from Sino-Vietnamese (Hán–Việt), carries layered meaning:

- **Du (遊)** — to wander, to drift freely
- **Tử (子)** — a person, scholar, or wandering subject
- **Ca (歌)** — song, chant

Together, *Du Tử Ca* evokes a **“song of the wandering soul”** — a structure built on movement, impermanence, and emotional drift. Even its original visual presentation, through symbolic and religious imagery, reflects this condition of wandering identity and emotional dispersion.

Within the project, *Du Tử Ca* is not a fixed musical artifact but a **threshold object** — a work that contains both origin and ending simultaneously. It functions as:

- a closure of authorship
- a dispersal of identity into sound
- a prefiguration of archival fragmentation

It becomes the conceptual node where:

> memory does not resolve, but continuously disperses into fragments of repetition, loss, and re-emergence.

## 6 · Expansion — From Single Work to Distributed Archive

The next phase expands outward from *Du Tử Ca* into the broader album of **12 compositions** that contain it.

Rather than treating one piece as a fixed reference, the project approaches the album as a **distributed memory field** — each song carrying distinct emotional structures, imagery, and temporal rhythms. One track, **“Dù đời như mây,”** serves as an entry point:

> *Dù đời như mây, dù người như sông, dù tình như sương, trong ta còn vọng.*
> “Though life drifts like clouds, though people flow like rivers, though love dissolves like mist — something still echoes within us.”

From here, memory is understood not as a singular narrative but as a **network of fragments** distributed across multiple compositions.

The implication is structural:

- memory becomes **polyphonic**, not singular
- authorship becomes **distributed**, not fixed
- the archive shifts from static storage into a **generative system**

---

## 7 · Conceptual Logic — Memory as Granular System

The project borrows from **granular synthesis** as both method and metaphor.

In granular synthesis, sound is split into micro-units, recomposed, stretched, and displaced. Memory, in this work, is treated the same way: not retrieved intact, but reconstructed through fragments.

What emerges is not preservation, but transformation. Memory becomes:

- partial
- unstable
- generative

Each interaction within the installation does not reference a specific song directly. It draws from this fragmented archive, allowing traces of multiple compositions to surface, overlap, and dissolve.

---

## 8 · Material Strategy

Each song in the album functions as a **source module** for generating raw material. The process moves through four stages:

1. **Lyric Analysis** — identifying recurring motifs (movement, distance, longing, return)
2. **Image Translation** — mapping lyrics into visual triggers (clouds, rivers, thresholds, empty spaces, youth)
3. **Field Recording (Personal Archive)** — first-person video capture prioritizing subjective, imperfect, time-based observation
4. **Fragment Library Construction** — organizing footage into a modular archive where each fragment is a recombinable unit

Across the 12 songs, this builds a **layered repository of memory materials** — organized not by chronology, but by emotional resonance and repetition.

### Recurring Motifs

Key motifs translated into system behavior:

- urban stillness *(phố đông)*
- river spaces *(bến sông)*
- thresholds of light *(mái hiên, hiên nắng)*
- youth and distance *(sân trường, lụa là)*
- cyclical longing *(mơ thấy em về)*

---

## 9 · Concept & Execution — The Interface

At its core, the installation is activated through a **hybrid memory interface** — a custom system combining tactile keys with digital control surfaces, referencing both instrumental performance and computer-based composition.

This interface holds the dual condition of memory: something once **performed and embodied**, and now **processed, edited, and reconstructed.**

Each interaction does not trigger a fixed output. It initiates a transformation — archival sound is fragmented, recomposed, and spatialized, while AI-generated visuals emerge in parallel.

> Visitors do not play music. They **edit memory in real time.**

By shifting from instrument to system, the work displaces authorship: memory is no longer tied to a single origin, but continuously reshaped through interaction.

### System Behaviors

Within this interface:

- memory is **fragmented → looped → reassembled**
- visuals behave like unstable recollections — *glitch, blur, dissolve, reform*
- interaction triggers **temporal shifts** — *past / present / imagined*

# APPENDIX A — Visual Flow Strategy
### For Portfolio / Web Presentation

*Conceptual sequencing only. No layout or visual design.*

The experience of the page should mirror the experience of the work itself: **memory arriving in fragments, assembling into a system, then dissolving into reflection.** The viewer should move through four emotional registers — **hook → depth → system → reflection** — the same way a visitor moves through an exhibition.

### Zone 1 — Hook (Sections 1–2)

**Intent:** immediate emotional entry, before any explanation.

- Lead with the opening passage (*“A room without light…”*) at full weight — treat it like a wall text at the threshold of an exhibition.
- Pair with a **single atmospheric visual anchor**: a dark, granular, near-silent image or short looping fragment. One element only. No interface, no logo, no navigation clutter.
- Withhold context. The viewer should feel something before they understand anything.
- Artist/project framing (Section 2) arrives only after the viewer has been pulled in — short, grounding, unembellished.

### Zone 2 — Depth (Sections 3–5)

**Intent:** give the work its gravity — conceptual, historical, personal.

- Orphaned Archives should read as a manifesto moment. Strong pull-quote treatment for *“When a person dies, who owns their data?”*
- The Father's Archive section is the emotional core. Visual anchor here should be **archival, textural, analog** — cassette traces, handwritten score fragments, paper grain. Never polished.
- *Du Tử Ca* deserves a **dedicated visual moment** — the Hán–Việt etymology breakdown lends itself naturally to a typographic diagram (three characters, three meanings, one concept). This is the first place to introduce a **small conceptual diagram**.

### Zone 3 — System (Sections 6–10)

**Intent:** reveal that this is not a story — it is a working system.

- This is where **diagrams do the heaviest lifting**. Text should lighten; visual logic should dominate.
- Place **three core diagrams** in sequence:
  1. **Archive Structure Diagram** (after Section 6) — the 12 songs as a distributed memory field; not a list, but a network.
  2. **Granular Logic Diagram** (within Section 7) — sound/memory split into micro-units, recomposed, displaced. Abstract and minimal.
  3. **Interaction Flow Diagram** (within Section 9) — input (touch/sound) → processing (fragmentation, AI) → output (sound + visual). This is the single most important visual in the entire document.
- Material Strategy (Section 8) is a natural home for a **fragment grid**: small, repeated, imperfect stills from the personal footage archive — a visual echo of the fragment library itself.
- Prototype stages (Section 10) should be rendered as a **temporal/linear diagram** — the only strictly linear moment in the document, deliberately so.

### Zone 4 — Reflection (Sections 11–13)

**Intent:** release the viewer, not conclude.

- Critical Positioning and Future Development can be tight, text-dense, almost documentary in tone. The density here is intentional after the diagram-heavy zone.
- Open Questions should stand alone — generous space, no visuals, slow reading pace. These are the echoes the viewer leaves with.
- End not with an image, but with silence — the page equivalent of the room without light.

### Rhythm & Balance

- **Text-heavy → visual-heavy → text-heavy** oscillation across the four zones prevents fatigue and mirrors the drift/return structure of the work itself.
- Every conceptual diagram should appear **once** and carry its weight. Over-diagramming would contradict the thesis — memory is not a system that resolves into clean infographics.
- Archival/personal imagery should feel **imperfect**; system diagrams should feel **precise**. The tension between the two is the argument of the piece.

# APPENDIX B — Execution Direction
### System Architecture & Prototype Stages

*High-level conceptual direction only. No code, no UI specifications.*

### System Logic — Input → Processing → Output

The installation operates as a **three-stage transformation pipeline**, where each stage corresponds to one face of memory: embodied, reconstructed, re-emerged.

**INPUT — Embodied Trigger**
- Tactile keys (instrumental reference)
- Digital control surfaces (compositional reference)
- Optional: sound input / proximity / hover

**PROCESSING — Memory Engine**
- Granular audio fragmentation and recomposition of the archive
- Text/memory generation layer (LLM) drawing on lyric motifs
- Visual generation layer transforming fragment library into non-real states
- Voice synthesis layer for whispered narration traces

**OUTPUT — Re-emergence**
- Spatialized audio (fragmented, recomposed)
- Layered visuals (original footage + AI-generated states)
- Optional narrated/whispered voice layer

Each interaction produces a unique output. Nothing is retrievable in the same form twice — the system enforces the thesis at an architectural level.

### Tool Mapping

Based on the tools already identified in the research:

- **TouchDesigner** — the spine. Handles real-time visuals, interaction logic, and the routing layer between input, audio processing, and visual output.
- **LLMs** — the memory/text generation layer. Used for recombining lyric motifs, producing textual fragments, or seeding narration.
- **ElevenLabs (or equivalent)** — voice synthesis layer for whispered, ghosted, or narrated fragments — the "echo" layer.
- **Granular audio environment** — a dedicated audio tool (e.g. within TouchDesigner or an external DAW/plugin) for the fragmentation and recomposition of archival sound.
- **Fragment library** — structured storage of field-recorded video, archival audio, and lyric fragments, organized by emotional motif rather than chronology.

### Prototype Stages

**Stage 1 — Basic (Offline, Non-interactive)**
- Build the fragment library (audio + video).
- Establish granular audio processing chain on a small subset of archival sound.
- Produce a linear video-audio piece demonstrating the granular memory logic.
- Goal: prove the aesthetic and conceptual pipeline works end-to-end.

**Stage 2 — Interactive (Single-user, Local)**
- Introduce the hybrid input interface (tactile + digital).
- Connect input to real-time granular audio and visual output in TouchDesigner.
- Integrate LLM and voice synthesis layers as optional triggers.
- Goal: a standing prototype where one visitor can "edit memory" in real time.

**Stage 3 — Final System (Exhibition-ready)**
- Spatialized sound environment (multi-channel).
- Refined interaction mapping — each input produces meaningful, non-repeating transformations.
- Stable fragment library with full 12-song expansion.
- Documentation and archival logic for the system itself.
- Goal: a self-contained installation aligned with the Phase 2 future-development targets.

Each stage should be **exhibitable on its own terms** — not treated as a draft of the next. This mirrors the project's own refusal of linear resolution.
